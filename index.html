Presenting,
Planting Your Way

Hey there! So, you're thinking about starting your own home garden? That's fantastic! Gardening is such a rewarding activity – whether you have a small balcony, a patch of lawn, or even just a few pots on a windowsill, there's something magical about watching things grow. Now, I know it can seem a bit overwhelming at first, but don't worry! I'm here to walk you through it.

Seeding Periods: Timing Is Everything

One of the first things to understand when starting a garden is timing. Different plants have different seeding periods, and getting the timing right is key for success. Here’s a general rule of thumb:

  • Spring Planting (March to May): Sow vegetables, herbs, and annual flowers like tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuce, peas, and carrots.
  • Autumn Planting (September to October): Plant overwintering crops like garlic, broad beans, and onions.
  • Summer Sowing (June to August): Great for fast-growing crops like radishes, salad greens, and beetroot.
  • Winter Planting: Hardy plants like broad beans or winter lettuces can be started in November or December.

Remember, always check the seed packet for specific planting times as varieties may differ slightly.

What to Grow? Think About Your Space and Taste!

Before planting, take a moment to think about your space and what you'd enjoy growing (and eating!). Here are a few suggestions depending on what you might like:

1. Herbs

If you're working with limited space or want an easy start, herbs like basil, parsley, coriander, and rosemary are perfect. They don’t need much room, and they’re great for adding fresh flavors to your cooking. Most herbs prefer spring sowing.

2. Vegetables

If you have a garden patch or raised bed, vegetables are super satisfying to grow. Think of things like tomatoes, cucumbers, courgettes (zucchini), beans, carrots, and lettuce. Sow indoors in March or April and transfer outside after frost.

3. Flowers

For a bit of color, why not add some flowers? Sunflowers, marigolds, pansies, and sweet peas are lovely to start with. Sow them in April or May for best results.

4. Fruit

For something more challenging, consider growing strawberries, raspberries, or even apple trees if you have space. Strawberries can be started in early spring from seeds or young plants.

5. Salad Greens

Salad greens like rocket, spinach, and lettuces are quick-growing and low-maintenance. They can be sown in early spring and again in late summer.

Tip: Start small. Pick a few things that excite you, and expand over time as you get more confident.

Choosing the Right Seeds: Personal Seed Brands Recommendations

When it comes to seeds, quality really does matter. The good news is that the UK has some amazing seed companies. Here are Some brands that I recommend:

  • Thompson & Morgan – Massive range of vegetable, flower, and herb seeds. Innovative and reliable.
  • Suttons Seeds – Over 200 years of experience, offering a wide variety including organic options.
  • Mr Fothergill’s – High-quality seeds and great customer service.
  • Real Seeds – Specializes in open-pollinated varieties, great for eco-conscious gardeners.
  • Kings Seeds – Affordable with over 130 years of experience.
  • Chiltern Seeds – Offers unusual flowers, herbs, and vegetables.

A Few Final Tips

  • Start Indoors: For seeds needing warmth, like tomatoes or peppers, start them indoors or on a sunny windowsill.
  • Label Everything: Use plant markers to avoid confusion once seedlings sprout.
  • Water Wisely: Ensure plants get enough water, but don’t overwater.
  • Be Patient: Gardening takes time, but it's worth the wait.

And there you have it – a simple guide to starting your own home garden! Happy gardening, and remember – the best time to plant a seed is today!"
